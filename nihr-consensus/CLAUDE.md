### Scheme aims

Develop new guidance on biomedical and health and social care research methods.

- Based on completed methodological research, the guidance should aim to improve the understanding and use of methods within the remit of MRC-NIHR Better Methods, Better Research Programme; and be accessible to those without specialist methodology knowledge

### Background

- model-based forecasting is increasingly used for real-time infectious disease surveillance and response
- UKHSA has invested in forecasting capacity but with no guidance on how to evaluate their work
- there is some evidence of forecasting within NHS trusts and public health intelligence teams (winter pressures, NHS EAD challenge)
- our team has developed leading forecast evaluation methods and software
- UKHSA have actively sought our guidance on forecast evaluation
- we propose formalising a consensus-based standardised set of forecast metrics to improve the understanding and use of appropriate forecast evaluation in operational public health settings

More detailed background could include

- standardised reporting protocols (PRISMA, GRADE) enable systematic review of reliable decision-grade evidence
- there is no such standardised process for evaluating or reporting forecasts
- there are multiple metrics available to evaluate forecasts, of which any may be more or less appropriate for different use cases
- for example, in aggregating across forecast targets, or in accounting for uncertainty of probabilistic forecasts
- there is evidence of inconsistent uptake of forecast evaluation metrics (e.g. UKHSA use relative WIS, NHS EAD challenge measured by MAE)
- we have actively developed software that supports validated methods for forecast evaluation
- this needs to be matched with user needs analysis and consensus on appropriate use of different metrics for different forecast use cases
- this will allow us to create guidance on appropriate forecast evaluation that will increase both understanding and uptake of evaluation methods

### Aims

With this funding, we wish to 

- develop a set of consensus guidelines on evaluating forecasting models used for operational public health intelligence
- scope is population-level forecasts, not clinical diagnostic/prognostic prediction
- users may be NHS trusts, public health intelligence teams, UKHSA
- we plan to recruit a panel of methodologists, public health specialists in local authorities, NHS trusts, and UKHSA
